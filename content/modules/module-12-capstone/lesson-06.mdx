# Lesson 6 - Demo, Retrospective, and Launch Readiness

The final presentation is not a sales pitch. It is an engineering review. You should show what works, what fails, what you measured, and what you would do next.

## Final demo structure

Use this structure:

1. User problem and scope
2. Architecture
3. Live or recorded run
4. Evaluation results
5. Safety and security controls
6. Observability and cost
7. UX decisions
8. Known limitations
9. Launch decision
10. Next iteration

## Launch-readiness decision

Choose one:

- Ready for limited production: safe for a small real audience with monitoring
- Ready for supervised pilot: useful, but human review required
- Internal demo only: promising but not reliable or controlled enough
- Not ready: major flaws block use

There is no shame in "supervised pilot" or "internal demo only." Honest readiness calls are a sign of engineering maturity.

## Retrospective prompts

Answer:

- What was the most important design decision?
- What assumption turned out wrong?
- Which evaluation case changed the system most?
- Which failure mode remains most worrying?
- What did you intentionally leave out?
- What would you build next?

<Callout type="key">
A strong capstone does not hide limitations. It names them, measures them, and explains what control would be required before a wider launch.
</Callout>
